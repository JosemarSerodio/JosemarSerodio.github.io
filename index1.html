WELCOME GUEST, HOW YOU FEELING ?

CUBS

Age range: 7 to 11 years
Cubs are the youngest members of the Scout movement. Through a fun program of games and activities both indoors and outdoors the youngsters learn a variety of skills. These include especially social skills relating to their family and friends, personal cleanliness, responsability and self - reliance. By working for the different advancement and interest badges they learn to take responsability for their own progress.

SCOUTS

Age range: 11 to 18 years
The Scout Programme is aimed to assist our youth in the difficult transition from childhood to adulthood. This is achieved through challeging, adventurous outdoor activities, in which the scouts are also learning basic leadership skills. This programme is supported by a progressive advancement programme, focusing on varius useful skills, but also social issues like gender, HIV/AIDS, drugs abuse, tolerance to other cultures, religions,etc.

ROVERS

Age range: 18 to 26 years
As young adults, Rovers are very sociable and highly dynamic, exploring options for the future. The Rover programme caters for this they are able to shape and plan their activities and progress according to their own needs, Strong emphasis is placed on social interactions with friends both within the rover crews and on a national and international basis and service to both the Scout Movement and the community.
